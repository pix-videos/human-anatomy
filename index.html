Overview

The heart is a muscular organ roughly the size of a closed fist. It sits in the chest, slightly left of center, and functions as the body's circulatory pump.

Key Statistics

300g Average Weight
100k Beats per Day
5L Blood per Minute
4 Chambers

Primary Functions

  • Pumps oxygenated blood throughout the body
  • Returns deoxygenated blood to the lungs
  • Maintains blood pressure
  • Regulates circulation speed based on activity

Anatomical Structures

Left Ventricle

Pumps blood to the body through the aorta

Right Ventricle

Pumps blood to the lungs for oxygenation

Atria

Upper chambers receiving blood flow
